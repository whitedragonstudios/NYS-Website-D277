Kingston

Kingston is a city in and the county seat of Ulster County, New York, United States. The population was 24,069 at the 2020 census, while the Kingston metropolitan statistical area had 177,749 residents. It is at the confluence of Rondout Creek and the Hudson River, 91 miles north of New York City and 59 miles south of Albany. The city's metropolitan area is grouped with the New York metropolitan area around Manhattan by the United States Census Bureau.
The city was named for Kingston upon Hull, England, which was the hometown of early settler and prominent landowner Thomas Chambers. It was also known as Esopus after the local Lenape tribe. The city served as the first capital of New York from 1777 to 1797. During the American Revolutionary War, it was burned by the British on October 16, 1777, after the Battles of Saratoga. As early as 1614, the Dutch had set up a trading post at the junction of the Rondout Creek and the Hudson River. In 1658, Peter Stuyvesant ordered the consolidation and fortification on high ground in what today is Uptown Kingston.
The stockade was a defense against the Esopus tribe, which had burned the village on several occasions. The Dutch and Esopus signed a peace treaty in 1665. A new stockade was built in 1673 and remained until 1694. By then, it had become more of a prison than a defensive fortification. Kingston was chartered as a village in 1669 and as a city in 1827. Kingston became New York's first capital in 1777. During the American Revolutionary War, the city was burned by the British on October 16, 1777, after the Battles of Saratoga. Albany was designated the state capital in 1797, years after the colonies achieved independence.
In the 19th century, Kingston became an important transport hub after the discovery of natural cement in 1825 in the region. The city's connections to other markets through both the railroad and Erie Canal made it strong economically. Today many of the older buildings associated with this era are considered contributing as part of three historic districts, including the Stockade District uptown, the Midtown Neighborhood Broadway Corridor, and the Rondout-West Strand Historic District downtown. Each district is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
